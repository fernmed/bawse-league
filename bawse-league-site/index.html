01How it works

We stop drafting players and start drafting entire teams — from MLB, NBA, NHL, NFL, college football, college basketball, and the Premier League. If a team you own wins its championship, you get paid. That's the whole game.

🐍

One snake draft

11 rounds, 110 picks, held every July during the MLB All-Star break — the only week all seven sports are asleep at once.

🏆

Seven trophies pay

The pot splits into 7 equal shares — World Series, NBA Finals, Stanley Cup, Super Bowl, CFP title, March Madness, and the Premier League.

🛋️

Zero maintenance

No lineups, no waivers, no Tuesday panic. Your total time cost is about 3 hours a YEAR — the draft plus trash talk.

🃏

The wildcard pick

Once per league, someone can draft “ALL UNDRAFTED” and own every leftover team. Every pick after that weakens their haul — live, in front of them.

🔄

Trades stay open

Trade anything, anytime — even a team that's IN its championship series. Lopsided trades aren't vetoed. They're content.

🥃

The shot rule

Lose your last team in a league? You owe the chat a video of you taking a shot within 48 hours. That's the entire punishment system.

02The seven championships

Every one of these 647 teams can be owned. Every title pays 1/7th of the pool. There's a live championship race in every month of the league year.

    07FAQ

    Do I have to know anything about soccer / college hoops / hockey?

    No — that's half the fun. The odds cheatsheet tells you who's good, and by February you'll be inexplicably screaming at Premier League matches you had no stake in last year.

    What happens to the teams nobody drafts?

    Each league's leftovers can be claimed as one "ALL UNDRAFTED" bundle pick during the draft. That means every team that could possibly win a title is owned by someone — no champion goes unclaimed.

    How is this less work than fantasy football?

    There are no lineups, no waivers, and no scoring to check. After the draft, your only possible move all year is a trade. Total commitment: one draft night plus however much trash talk you choose to generate.

    What if my teams are all garbage by January?

    Then you take your shot-rule videos with dignity and start working the trade market. Contenders get expensive at the deadline — sell your last hopes to a desperate rival for next year's draft position.

    Is this replacing our fantasy football league?

    That's the proposal on the table: we're resetting anyway, and this keeps the group competing year-round instead of going dark in January. Vote is open in the group chat — draft night is in two weeks.

    Where did this format come from?

    A league on r/FFCommish that's run it happily for 2+ years, plus another group that copied it and reported back a year later still loving it. We adapted their rules for 10 managers.
